\section{\sectiontitleII}
\frame{
\frametitle{Independent and Dependent Variables}

{``If a change in one variable will not affect the value of some other variable, the
two are considered independent of each other. A variable that can be changed independently of other
variables is known as an \hspcu\hspcu. A variable that is affected by changes in one or more
other variables is known as a \hspcu\hspcu. Normally, the variable that we measure depends on
the value of the variables that control the process.''} \vspc
{\tiny Text: Theory and Design of Mech. Meas.}

}

% Section 3
\section{\sectiontitleIII}
\frame{
\frametitle{\sectiontitleIII}

{``A variable is \hspcu if it can be held at a constant value
or at some prescribed condition during a measurement... ...complete control of a variable is not usually
possible. We use the adjective \hspcu to refer to a variable that can be held as prescribed, at
least in a nominal sense... \vspc
...we define a \hspcu as a functional grouping of variables. For example, a moment of inertia or a Reynolds number... ...A \hspcu that has an effect on the behavior of the measured variable is called a \hspcu \hspcu....''} \vspc
{\tiny Text: Theory and Design of Mech. Meas.}

}

% Section 4
\section{\sectiontitleIV}
\frame{
\frametitle{\sectiontitleIV}

{``Variables that are not or cannot be controlled during measurement but that affect the value of the
variable measured are called \hspcu\hspcu. Their influence can confuse the clear relation
between cause and effect in a measurement... ...The effects due to \hspcu\hspcu can take the form of signals superimposed
onto the measured signal with such forms as {\PR noise} and drift.''} \vspc
{\tiny Text: Theory and Design of Mech. Meas.}

}
